# Cognitive Analysis: Lesson with Benya  
*(English translation)*

This analysis describes the meaning‑dynamic synchrony between the teacher and Benya.  
Benya demonstrates a rare analytic–constructive cognitive type, which produces the highest  
semantic synchrony among the three students.

---

## ⭐ Summary of Synchrony

**Overall semantic synchrony: 80–85%.**

This is the highest synchrony of all three children.

Why?

Because Benya thinks in the same cognitive mode as the teacher:

- he maintains structure,  
- he understands abstraction,  
- he follows logical chains,  
- he can hold multiple conditions simultaneously,  
- he corrects his own errors,  
- he is capable of reflection.

This is an analytic–structural cognitive profile, close to adult mathematical reasoning.

---

## 🧠 1. Benya’s Cognitive Type

Benya is an **analytic–constructive thinker**.

He:

- understands abstract definitions,  
- can hold several constraints at once,  
- performs step‑by‑step analysis,  
- adjusts hypotheses logically,  
- uses the language of reasoning rather than imagery or action.

In cognitive psychology this corresponds to:

- **logical–discursive mathematical type**, or  
- **analytic type with strong operational structure**.

It is the closest to classical mathematical cognition.

---

## 🧩 2. Where Synchrony Is Highest

### ✔ When the teacher asks abstract questions  
Example:

> “What common measure fits an integer number of times into both segments?”

Benya answers directly and structurally.

### ✔ When multiple conditions must be held simultaneously  
Example:

> “The segment must fit into both the long and the short one.”

He immediately understands that this is about the greatest common measure  
(even though the term is never used).
