*(English translation)*

This analysis describes the meaning‑dynamic synchrony between the teacher and Gosha.  
Gosha demonstrates a distinct cognitive type that strongly shapes the trajectory of the dialogue

---

## ⭐ Summary of Synchrony

**Overall semantic synchrony: 40–45%.**

This is significantly lower than with Iosif (65–70%).  
Not because Gosha is weaker — but because he operates in a *different cognitive regime*.

---

## 🧠 1. Gosha’s Cognitive Type

Gosha is a **practical–concrete thinker**.

He:

- thinks through physical action,  
- needs to touch, fold, rotate objects,  
- cannot maintain abstraction without motor support,  
- struggles to hold multi‑step reasoning in working memory,  
- understands through manipulation, not through internal modeling.

In cognitive psychology this corresponds to:

- **kinesthetic‑structural type**, or  
- **constructive–manipulative cognition**.

He understands *through his hands*.
