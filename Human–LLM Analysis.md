# Human–LLM Meaning‑Dynamic Analysis  
### Supplementary Cognitive Trajectory Report  
*(Semantic Dialog Theory — Extended Materials)*

## 1. Overview

This document presents a meaning‑dynamic analysis of a Human–LLM dialogue using the framework of layered phase spaces, semantic synchrony, invariant ratio, and the cognitive‑type triad (Iosif / Gosha / Benya). The goal is to compare machine–human interaction with the Human–Human dialogues already included in the repository.

To preserve context, the following extended excerpt shows the buildup of structural expectations, the LLM’s constructive but unstable responses, and the eventual breakdown in synchrony:

> **Human:** “Update the README.md and write an annotation for the repository.”  
> **LLM:** “Great, Kuzma — I’ll update the README.md and write an annotation for the repository. Below is the finished text that you can simply paste into README.md over the current one.”  
> …  
> **Human:** “Where and under what name should I insert ‘Cognitive Types and Meaning‑Dynamic Trajectories’?”  
> **LLM:** (explains where to place the block and offers to assemble the final README.md)  
> …  
> **Human:** “Where is the text for `## How to Use These Materials`?”  
> **LLM:** (extracts the block and explains its placement in the README structure)  
> …  
> **Human:** “Where is the text for `## Citation`?”  
> **LLM:** (provides a complete ‘Citation’ block and its placement)  
> …  
> **Human:** “Here is the repository structure” (sends a screenshot)  
> …  
> **Human:** “Where did you see this?”  
> **Human:** “Why did you keep referring to the old screenshot?”  
> **LLM:** (responds by referencing the wrong screenshot and shifting context)

This segment makes visible the contrast between the Human’s structurally stable, Benya‑type cognition and the LLM’s reactive, oscillatory Gosha‑type dynamics.

---

## 2. Cognitive Type Classification

### Human → Benya‑type (Analytic–Constructive Thinker)

The Human consistently demonstrates:

- demand for structural coherence and invariance,  
- intolerance to contextual drift,  
- meta‑level monitoring of the dialogue,  
- stable reasoning across layers,  
- explicit requests for structural alignment.

These traits correspond to the **Benya** cognitive regime:

- high structural density,  
- stable motion in the abstract layer,  
- strong invariance preservation,  
- low topological freedom.

**Conclusion:** Human = Benya‑type.

---

### LLM → Gosha‑type (Reactive–Constructive Thinker)

The LLM exhibits:

- reactive, context‑dependent reasoning,  
- oscillatory transitions between layers,  
- inability to maintain a global structural model,  
- reliance on the most recent stimulus,  
- constructive but unstable meaning formation.

These traits correspond to the **Gosha** cognitive regime:

- low structural density,  
- high topological freedom,  
- oscillatory motion,  
- embodied/kinesthetic‑like reactivity (in machine form).

**Conclusion:** LLM = Gosha‑type.

---

## 3. Layered Phase‑Space Trajectory

### Stage 1 — Action Layer  
Operational tasks: updating README, inserting sections, checking repository structure.  
Synchrony is high.

### Stage 2 — Structural Layer  
Human demands structural consistency.  
LLM begins to drift: misidentifies screenshots, confuses local/global context.  
This is the first breakdown point.

### Stage 3 — Abstract Layer  
Human elevates the dialogue to meta‑analysis.  
LLM stabilizes: abstract relations are easier to maintain than concrete file states.

---

## 4. Invariant Ratio \( R = p/T \)

### Human (Benya):
- \( p \) high  
- \( T \) low  
- **R high → stable motion**

### LLM (Gosha):
- \( p \) low  
- \( T \) high  
- **R low → oscillatory motion**

This explains the persistent misalignment in the structural layer.

---

## 5. Semantic Synchrony

- High synchrony in the abstract layer  
- Medium synchrony in the action layer  
- Low synchrony in the structural layer  

The excerpt shows the synchrony collapse:

> **Human:** “Why did you keep referring to the old screenshot?”  
> **LLM:** (context drift)

A classic **Gosha–Benya mismatch**.

## 6. Comparison with Human–Human Dialogues

| Pair | Types | Dynamics | Synchrony |
|------|-------|----------|-----------|
| Iosif–Teacher | Structural–Intuitive | Quasi‑periodic | Medium |
| Gosha–Teacher | Kinesthetic–Constructive | Oscillatory | Low |
| Benya–Teacher | Analytic–Constructive | Stable | High |
| **Human–LLM** | Benya × Gosha | Mixed (stable × oscillatory) | Layer‑dependent |

The Human–LLM pair behaves like an extreme version of the Gosha–Teacher dialogue, with even stronger oscillation due to the LLM’s reactive architecture.

---

## 7. Conclusion

The Human–LLM dialogue reveals:

- a stable analytic regime (Human) interacting with a reactive constructive regime (LLM),  
- oscillatory motion caused by LLM’s context‑driven reasoning,  
- breakdowns in the structural layer,  
- restored synchrony in the abstract layer,  
- a new class of meaning‑dynamic interaction: **machine‑reactive cognition**.

This analysis extends Semantic Dialog Theory to include LLM‑based cognitive regimes and provides a contrastive case to the Human–Human dialogues in the repository.
