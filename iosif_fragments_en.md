# Key Transcript Fragments: Lesson with Iosif  
*(English translation)*

These excerpts include only the parts of the lesson that are directly referenced in the cognitive analysis.  
They illustrate Iosif’s structural–intuitive cognitive style, his reliance on internal visual models,  
and the characteristic moments of semantic synchrony and desynchrony with the teacher.

---

## 1. Intuitive structural reasoning (“the bracket method”)

**Iosif:**  
“It’s easiest to count using these brackets.”  
“Like, by the little blocks — that’s the most natural.”

This fragment shows his preference for internal visual structures rather than formulas.

---

## 2. Difficulty with symbolic abstraction

**Teacher:**  
“What is *x* in your solution?”

**Iosif:**  
“Um… relative speed?”  
“No, wait… maybe *x* is 1.”  
“Or maybe *x* is Piero’s speed… I’m not sure.”

This illustrates his instability with symbolic systems — he does not anchor variables.

---

## 3. Insight through internal visualization

**Iosif:**  
“So Buratino made five brackets, and Piero made four.”  
“So Buratino arrives earlier.”

This is a pure structural insight: he sees the solution as a pattern of segments, not as algebra.

---

## 4. Loss of synchrony when the teacher formalizes

**Teacher:**  
“Half the path divided by *x*, a quarter divided by 1.2x…”

**Iosif:**  
“I don’t understand…  
I think I’m confused.”

This is a key moment used in the analysis:  
Iosif loses the thread when the reasoning becomes symbolic and multi‑step.

---

## 5. Return to synchrony when structure is restored

**Teacher:**  
“Show me with your brackets how they move.”

**Iosif:**  
“Here Piero is at the middle, and here Buratino is already here…”

He immediately regains clarity when allowed to use his internal visual model.

---

## 6. Non‑linear reasoning and skipping steps

**Iosif:**  
“So they arrive at the same time.”  
“Or maybe Buratino is faster…  
Wait, no — let’s check again.”

This fragment shows his tendency to jump to conclusions without linear justification.

---

## Summary

These fragments illustrate:

- Iosif’s structural–intuitive cognitive type  
- His reliance on internal visual models (“brackets”, “blocks”)  
- His difficulty with symbolic formalism  
- His non‑linear reasoning and tendency to skip steps  
- The points where semantic synchrony rises or collapses  

They correspond directly to the analysis in `iosif_analysis_en.md`.
