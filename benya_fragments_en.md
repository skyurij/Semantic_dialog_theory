# Key Transcript Fragments: Lesson with Benya  
*(English translation)*

These excerpts include only the parts of the lesson that are directly referenced in the cognitive analysis.  
They illustrate Benya’s analytic–constructive cognitive style, his ability to hold abstract structure,  
and the high degree of semantic synchrony with the teacher.

---

## 1. Abstract reasoning and structural thinking

**Teacher:**  
“What segment would you take now — one that fits an integer number of times into both segments?”

**Benya:**  
“I’d take this one…  
It fits once into the first segment, and two or three times into the second.”

This shows that Benya immediately grasps the structural requirement (common measure).

---

## 2. Recognition of the minimal common unit

**Benya:**  
“I’ve got it — the segment of length 1.  
It fits into both.”

This is the moment where he identifies the invariant (the minimal common unit),  
showing analytic clarity.

---

## 3. Ability to justify abstractly

**Teacher:**  
“Is there a longer segment that also fits an integer number of times?”

**Benya:**  
“No.  
Because 2 is even, and it cannot fit into 23 — an odd number.”

This is a fully abstract justification, not tied to concrete imagery.

---

## 4. Meta‑cognitive awareness

**Teacher:**  
“You need to say it more precisely, right?”

**Benya:**  
“Yes, I should be more precise.”

This shows his ability to reflect on the structure of reasoning —  
a hallmark of analytic‑constructive cognition.

---

## 5. Quick correction of hypotheses

**Benya:**  
“No, this measure won’t work…  
Then we need a slightly different one.”

He adjusts his reasoning without losing the structure —  
a sign of high cognitive flexibility.

---

## 6. Handling abstraction without concrete objects

**Teacher:**  
“Let’s move to numbers.”

**Benya:**  
“Okay.”

He transitions smoothly from geometric to numeric reasoning,  
showing that abstraction does not disrupt his cognitive flow.
